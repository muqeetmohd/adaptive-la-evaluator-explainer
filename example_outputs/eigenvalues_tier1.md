**User query:** What is an eigenvalue?

---

**Assistant (Tier 1 response):**

Imagine you have a machine that stretches and rotates arrows. Most arrows you feed into it come out pointing in a completely different direction — rotated, flipped, who knows.

But some special arrows come out pointing in **exactly the same direction** they started — they just got longer or shorter. Those special arrows are called **eigenvectors**.

The **eigenvalue** is simply the number that tells you *how much* that arrow got stretched or squished:
- Eigenvalue = 2 → the arrow doubled in length
- Eigenvalue = 0.5 → the arrow shrank to half its size
- Eigenvalue = -1 → the arrow flipped direction but stayed the same length

So an eigenvalue answers the question: "for this special arrow, how much did the transformation scale it?"
